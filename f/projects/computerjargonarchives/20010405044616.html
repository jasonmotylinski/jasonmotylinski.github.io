2001-03-29 15:15:25 - Fuck Napster

I am seriously pissed. Today the inevitable happened. I went searching Napster for the Deftones "Passenger" ala Maynard James Keenan and came up empty on the search. There were a few lingering Deftones songs but they were rarities and b-sides. Not what I was looking for. So I figured I would try my luck at other services popping up. I'm very familiar with Gnutella and the inherent flaws in it's system so I thought I would try another call iMesh. It's been touted as the most downloading client sharing program, second only to Napster. I gave it a whirl and let me tell you, it's not the second coming of Jesus.

Let's face it. What Napster was will never be again. Granted, we may be able to subscribe to a Napster like service in the not too distant future. But never again will audio trading exist like it did last year. And I'm pissed.

I think our only hope is Gnutella. I've played around with Gnutella and, more specifically, it's protocol. Gnutella is an interesting idea. Create a network of clients which talk to each other in a spiderweb fashion without a central server. Sounds like a winning idea, right? Remove the big brother layer of Napster. Plus Gnutella uses http headers for passing it's information. If ISPs were to block http headers then no one would be able to surf the web. So everything has been thought through with Gnutella right?

Log a bunch of high bandwidth users on and all is good on the Gnutella network. Throw in some modem users and the shit hits the fan. Imagine our computers are connected to the Internet through DSL. My computer asks your computer if it has the Deftones "Passenger" mp3. With a phat connection the request is pretty unnoticable. I would get a near immediate response. Now what if you were on a modem? What if the pipe between you and me wasn't as phat? Well, we still wouldn't see much of a lag. Now imagine if 10 people are asking you, on your little modem, if it has the Deftones "Passenger."
